Dating back to the Dutch East India Company, sending an employee to work in another country has carried considerable costs while theoretically opening the potential for financial returns for the employer. With tax equalization, housing allowance, cost-of-living adjustment and other benefits, the typical expatriate compensation package is two to three times the home-country base salary.
